Bachelor’s Thesis: Web Application for Renting Clothes for Special Events

This thesis focused on developing a sustainable platform designed for the rental of clothing, footwear, and accessories, while also reflecting on the current realities and the negative environmental impact of the fashion industry. The project was conceived as a responsible and economical alternative to the excessive consumption characteristic of this sector. It offers a digital solution that encourages reuse and aims to reduce unnecessary purchases, especially during special events, as well as the overuse of raw materials and chemical compounds, particularly those used for dyeing.

By implementing PHP/MySQL technologies for the back-end and HTML/CSS/JavaScript/Bootstrap for the user interface, the platform was designed to be accessible, intuitive, and efficient.

Its features include account creation, password reset functionality, filtering items by preference, adding products to a favorites list, shopping cart and user data management for order completion, temporary rentals, return requests (with additional fees for late returns), and an administration panel for catalog updates and return handling. These contribute to a clear, transparent, and automated process.

Overall, this digital solution demonstrates how technology can address real-world challenges when used to promote a responsible consumption model, meeting both the economic needs of users and the growing urgency of environmental protection.
